# YOUR NAME Human User Guide 
The goal of a human user guide is to make working together and collaboration with colleagues easier, faster. The focus for this user guide is on the workplace. As such, many of the questions are geared towards information that would be pertinent to your colleagues.

## About me

### What are some honest, unfiltered things about you?
- I am from India, I am from South, but mostly brought up in the South East part of India
- I like to talk a lot, and crack jokes which may not be funny at times :) 
	
### What drives you nuts?
- When people are disrespectful
- When people do not respect each other's time

### What are your quirks?
- I like to make fun of myself 
- Cannot stand long meetings where people talk ONLY about work

### What are some things that people might misunderstand about you that you should clarify?
- I am a warm person, and assume others to be the same. Hence I feel free to crack jokes be it any type of 
- When I am multi tasking, I tend to make some asks on slack without much context at times. Please feel free to ask for more details, if I am not clear. I am learning this art of async communication

## About my colleagues

### What qualities do you particularly value in your colleagues?
- I like when my colleagues trust and are honest to be core
- I prefer colleagues to tell me my areas of improvement on time with examples of my behaviour/context
- I like when my colleagues respect time, and meetings are time boxed with clear agenda

### What do you wish colleagues didn’t do?
- Just telling problems and passing the ball when it comes to take decision
- Not being honest with right status of work, risks, etc

### How can people earn an extra gold star with you?
- Being extremely honest and seek help
- Crack jokes, talk non-work stuff, get to know each other personally to be the best of colleagues

## About my interactions

### How do you coach people to do their best work and develop their talents?
- I share my personal journey with my colleagues to keep them motivated
- I am an extreme believer of situational leadership, I like to understand the person and the situation, and guide based on that
	
### What’s the best way to communicate with you?
- I prefer slack messages in the channel as much as possible, mention my handle if its urgent
- When we are not coming to an agreement withing few mins of slack communication, I prefer to hop onto a quick call
	
### What’s the best way to convince you to do something?
- Just be honest and let me know the deadline
	 
### How do you like to give feedback?
- Over any 1:1 meetings, and I prefer to give feedback ontime
- I also like to thank and appreciate my colleagues on time for their work/help/ideas

### How do you like to get feedback?
- I appreciate feedback in the form of oral communication during 1:1 meetings
- I prefer feedback given to me ontime with specific context
